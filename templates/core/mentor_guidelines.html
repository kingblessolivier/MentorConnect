Getting Started

Welcome to our mentoring community! Here's what you need to do to get started:

  1. Complete your profile: Add a professional photo, detailed bio, and list your areas of expertise
  2. Set your availability: Use the calendar to show when you're available for sessions
  3. Define your expertise: Clearly state what topics you can help with
  4. Set expectations: Specify session duration and any requirements

Mentor Responsibilities

Be Punctual

Respect your mentee's time by being on time for all scheduled sessions

Be Responsive

Respond to messages within 48 hours and keep communication open

Be Prepared

Review mentee's goals and prepare relevant advice before sessions

Be Supportive

Provide constructive feedback and encouragement

Best Practices

  • Listen actively: Understand your mentee's needs before offering advice
  • Share experiences: Use real-world examples from your career
  • Set goals together: Help mentees define clear, achievable objectives
  • Provide actionable advice: Give specific, practical steps they can take
  • Follow up: Check in on their progress between sessions
  • Be honest: Give genuine feedback, even when it's difficult

Communication Guidelines

Do

  • Use professional language
  • Be clear and concise
  • Ask clarifying questions
  • Acknowledge messages promptly
  • Provide context for advice

Don't

  • Share personal contact info initially
  • Make promises you can't keep
  • Discuss confidential information
  • Use inappropriate language
  • Ignore messages for extended periods

Session Guidelines

Make the most of your mentoring sessions:

1

Before the Session

Review the mentee's profile, previous notes, and prepare discussion topics

2

During the Session

Start with a check-in, discuss key topics, and leave time for questions

3

After the Session

Summarize key points, assign action items, and schedule the next meeting

Professional Boundaries

Maintaining professional boundaries is essential for a healthy mentoring relationship:

  • Keep conversations focused on professional development
  • Avoid giving advice on personal matters outside your expertise
  • Maintain confidentiality about mentee's information
  • Be aware of power dynamics and avoid exploiting them
  • Know when to refer mentees to other resources or professionals
