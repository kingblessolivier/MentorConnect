About Mirror

Bridging the Gap Between
Education & Reality

We believe that real-world experience is the key to making informed career decisions. Mirror connects students with professionals for observation internships that change futures.

Our Mission

More Information, Better Choices

At Mirror, we believe that more information leads to better study choices. That's why we organize observation internships of 1-5 days, where students literally become the shadow of a professional.

They follow their mentor in daily activities and experience the reality of the profession up close—an experience that is richer than any conversation, brochure, or career day could ever provide.

Key Principles

Observe, Don't Perform

Students are there to observe and learn, not to work or prove themselves.

Confidentiality First

Every student signs an NDA before their observation internship.

Honest & Realistic

Show both the positive and challenging aspects of every profession.

The Context

A Fundamental Problem

One of the most crucial decisions in a young adult's life is choosing a profession

The Problem

Many students make their choice of study at age 17 or 18 with only a vague—or even no—idea of what jobs they can do later. There are endless brochures and information days about study programmes, but hardly any ways to really discover what a job looks like day after day.

Choices are based on assumptions, expectations, and what they've seen on TV—often incorrect.

The Result

Young people start courses without knowing where they really lead, and therefore often lose motivation, time, and valuable credits along the way—or end up feeling stuck in a job that doesn't match who they are.

Many sectors face talent shortages while young people hesitate because they can't imagine what working there actually looks like.

The Root Cause

The problem is not their lack of motivation—it's a lack of real-world insight at the moment it matters most.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is Mirror for?

Mirror is for anyone who wants to get a more realistic picture of a profession. In practice, these are mainly pupils in the last years of secondary school and students in their bachelor's degree. However, Mirror is open to anyone who wants to inform themselves and thus make better choices for the future.

How long does an observation internship last?

An observation internship lasts between 1 and 5 working days. This gives you a diverse and realistic picture of the profession in a short time.

Do I need to have prior knowledge?

No, prior knowledge is not necessary. You are there to observe and learn, always under the guidance of the mentor.

What about confidential information?

Discretion and confidentiality are central to our observation internships. Every student signs a non-disclosure agreement (NDA) in advance, which strictly prohibits the sharing of confidential information, company data, or personal data.

What if the planned days don't fit?

Together we look for another time or an alternative mentor. Just get in touch and we will be happy to help you.
